## Design Context

### Users
Three audiences land on xtil.ai in one scroll:

1. **Curious web reader** — non-technical visitor from Reddit, HN, or a friend's link. Skeptical of "another AI tool." Decides in 10–15 seconds whether this is different. Needs immediate proof, not a feature list.
2. **Privacy-conscious professional** — researcher, journalist, analyst. Here to verify BYOK, open-source, no-account claims. Wants to see the comparison table and the repo before trusting a claim.
3. **Developer / power user** — evaluating for themselves and possibly recommending. Wants API provider support, source code, extension internals.

All three decide quickly. The page must serve the curious reader first but hold up under the pro's and dev's scrutiny.

**Job to be done**: Understand in 10 seconds what makes xTil different from "generic AI summarizers" — free, BYOK, privacy-first, genre-aware — then install it from the Chrome Web Store.

### Brand Personality
- **Three words**: *Considered, honest, literate.*
- **Voice**: Writes full sentences. No emoji in marketing copy. No "unlock," "supercharge," or "revolutionize." Plain words. Quiet confidence.
- **Emotional goal**: Trust through typographic craft, not trust through glow effects. A reader should feel the same way they feel reading a well-edited magazine feature: respected.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Tone**: Editorial / magazine. Think Stripe.press feature article, a New Yorker long-form, or a thoughtful personal essay site. Typography does the heavy lifting — size contrast, rhythm, asymmetry — not ornament.

**References (spiritually, not to copy)**: Stripe.press. Robinhood's annual letter. The Browser Company's manifesto posts. It's Nice That feature layouts. A well-set book interior.

**Anti-references**:
- NOT retro / skeuomorphic (no CRT, no 90s revival, no Win95 nostalgia)
- NOT clever-to-a-fault (not an art-project portfolio; it must convert)
- NOT corporate-bland (not a consulting white paper; must have a point of view)
- NOT the generic-AI-tool template (no violet/indigo, no orbs, no glow, no gradient text, no shimmer)

**Theme**: Auto — `prefers-color-scheme` respected. Light is canonical (warm off-white paper, near-black ink). Dark is a clean mirror with no glow added.

**Palette direction** (concrete tokens to use):
- Ink: warm near-black `oklch(15% 0.01 60)` ≈ `#1a1815`
- Paper: warm off-white `oklch(97% 0.01 80)` ≈ `#faf6ee`
- Mute: 40% ink on paper for secondary text
- Hairline: 10% ink for 1px dividers
- Accent: **iron-oxide red** `oklch(54% 0.14 30)` ≈ `#b04a34` — print-ink color, sparingly used (≤8% of surface), replaces all purple
- Dark-mode ink: `oklch(94% 0.01 80)` on `oklch(13% 0.005 60)` background, accent shifted to `oklch(62% 0.13 30)`

**Typography** (neither font appears on the reflex-reject list):
- **Display**: Gambarino Variable (Fontshare, free). High-contrast editorial serif with unusual character — distinct without being decorative.
- **Body**: Supreme Variable (Fontshare, free). Refined neo-grotesque with subtle humanist warmth. Pairs against Gambarino cleanly.
- Both are free for commercial use. Host locally rather than CDN-linking to avoid layout shift.
- Fallback stack: `Gambarino, "Iowan Old Style", Georgia, serif` for display; `Supreme, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", sans-serif` for body.

### Design Principles
1. **Typography carries hierarchy, not color.** Size, weight, and spacing do the work. Save color for a single disciplined accent.
2. **Hairlines over shadows.** A 1px divider at low opacity replaces the default AI drop-shadow-on-rounded-rect. Cards get borders, not box-shadows.
3. **Asymmetry is the grid.** 7-5 splits, full-bleed pull-quotes, ragged-right card runs. No centered 3-column bento unless it's the only option.
4. **One accent, rarely.** Iron-oxide red shows up on the primary CTA, one underline, one highlight span. Never on text fill, never as a gradient.
5. **Ornament must earn its keep.** No orbs, no noise, no shimmer, no glow. If a decoration doesn't carry meaning (a pull-quote mark, a caption rule), it doesn't ship.

### Rebrand Scope (locked)
- Free fonts only. Gambarino + Supreme (Fontshare). No paid alternatives to be introduced in later steps.
- Wordmark can be redrawn in Gambarino or a custom display setting.
- Scope flows to: landing page (index.html + changelog.html) → favicon.svg + icon.svg + promo art (`website/public/store/*`) → extension sidepanel UI (`src/entrypoints/sidepanel/`). Website is step-one; extension UI follows once landing is locked.
